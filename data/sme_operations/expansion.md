# Expansion readiness (SME operations)

> Public, challenge-safe placeholder notes. No customer or private data.

## Concept

Expanding from one location to two is where informal, trust-based operations get
stress-tested. The question is not "can we afford it?" alone — it is whether the
operator's records, staff, cash discipline, and demand base can survive being
split across two sites.

## Readiness factors to evaluate

- **Records:** Are cash records consistent enough to compare two locations?
- **Staff:** Is there a second trusted person who can run a site independently?
- **Demand:** Is demand stable/seasonal in a predictable way, or volatile?
- **Working capital:** Is there enough to fund stock and float for two sites
  before the second site breaks even?
- **Supplier reach:** Can existing suppliers serve the second location?

## Risks to weigh

- The first location weakens while the owner focuses on the second.
- Cash discipline breaks when the owner cannot watch both sites.
- Seasonal demand hides whether the second location is viable year-round.
- Working capital gets trapped and neither site is properly stocked.

## What to avoid

- Expanding on a single strong season's results.
- Expanding without a trusted second in command.
- Expanding while cash records are too irregular to compare locations.
